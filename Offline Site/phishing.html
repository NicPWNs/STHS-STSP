Phishing Attacks

When it comes to phshing attacks, I find them to be the easiest for attackers to pull off, as well as the easiest for victims to fall for. Here is my analysis of phishing, and ways to avoid it.

The definition of phishing is as follows: the activity of defrauding an online account holder of financial information by posing as a legitimate company. To me I see phishing as much more than this. Attackers that use phishing can do it for many more reasons than just the money. It can be for a retrieval of information for a much larger hacking scheme, or even just for social purposes or blackmail. Phishing is very dangerous on the user level of cybersecurity, simply because the general population is not very educated on identifying phishing scams. Here is my set of rules to identifying and trusting sites/emails.

  • The URL: Always be sure to check the URL of a site very careully to be sure that it is the site you want to be on, sometimes attackers may slip a single little character in the middle of the URL, and until you look carefully it may seem legitimate.
  • HTTPS: If a website is trusted and certified to be secure, rather than saying HTTP, it will say HTTPS. Some browsers may even show a lock or green check in the URL box at the top.
  • Apps: Some apps, especially social networking apps have built in browsers that do not show the full URL of the website you are on. Attackers will use this mechanic to trick even aware victims into a phishing site.
  • Emails: Always be sure that emails recieved are from a verified domain. Just like the URL, be sure that the @example.com is the one you think that the email should be from. Attackers can make very believeable emails.
